What is GDG?

Google Developer Groups (GDG) are community groups for college and university students interested in Google developer technologies. Students from all undergraduate or graduate programs with an interest in growing as a developer are welcome. By joining a GDG, students grow their knowledge in a peer-to-peer learning environment and build solutions for local businesses and their community.

▶

How can you become a part of GDG?

We conduct an annual team recruitment process. The details of recruitment are posted online a few weeks prior. It’s a two-step process involving form filling and a personal interview. Students acing both rounds are selected to be part of the core team.

▶

What does a GDG Lead do?

A GDG Lead fills many roles. They work with the university to build the club, recruit the core team, host workshops, build projects, and collaborate with local partners.

▶

How is DSC related to Google?

DSC or Developer Student Clubs are community groups for college or university students supported by Google. The students try out new ideas and collaborate to solve various development problems.
